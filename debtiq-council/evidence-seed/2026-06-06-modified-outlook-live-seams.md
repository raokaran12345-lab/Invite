# Evidence — Modifying the outlook: the live seams (2026-06-06)

Type: strategy synthesis grounded in the prior research + two validation searches.
Purpose: the bearish files map the CURRENT board (point-tools owned by Quickli/
Fortiro/CRMs). This file documents what those files do NOT prove — that the
category is shifting, and in the shift there are winnable seams. Each is a
HYPOTHESIS with a why-it-could-win and an explicit KILL criterion. Not copium:
every seam is also a race with a clock.

## What the research actually killed (keep honest)
Only the naïve thesis: "a standalone point tool beats Quickli/Fortiro head-on in
the OLD category (serviceability calc / doc fraud)." That is dead. [high]
It did NOT prove the category is static or that no seam exists.

## What just changed the picture (two findings)
1. The market is moving from point-tools to AGENTIC, end-to-end assessment — and
   no AU player clearly owns the unified, broker-side, compliance-grade agent yet.
   Early movers exist (Aline Tech automation; Bulma policy intelligence ~ like
   Quickli's Jiffi; global AWS Bedrock mortgage agents) but it's fragmented; Quickli
   is still a calculator, Fortiro still fraud, CRMs still workflow. White space is
   NARROWING, not closed. [medium]
2. CDR "action initiation" (WRITE access) became law Aug 2024 — it explicitly
   enables "automating the processes for undertaking loan or mortgage applications,"
   alongside the 2026 non-bank-lender data rollout. A CDR-native agent that pulls
   verified data AND initiates the application is a genuine forward frontier
   incumbents built on read-only OCR must retrofit. [high that the law exists;
   medium on timing/usefulness]

## The live seams (ranked) — each with a kill criterion
1. **The unified end-to-end agent (connective tissue nobody owns).**
   Documents → verified (forensics) → serviceable (multi-lender) → auto-generated
   BID/responsible-lending evidence, in ONE agentic pass. Each incumbent is locked
   to ONE category AND ONE channel (Fortiro can't sell to brokers; Quickli won't
   take compliance liability; CRMs can't do real forensics) — combining crosses
   lines they won't. DebtIQ already has all four pieces built.
   KILL: if 5 brokers say "I'd rather keep Quickli + my CRM separate," it's dead.
2. **CDR-native + action initiation (the forward bet).**
   Be native to the 2026 rails: open-banking data where it exists, forensics for
   the alt-doc gap, auto-application via write-access. Incumbents must retrofit.
   KILL: if NextGen Financial Passport / Frollo already own the broker CDR+initiation
   flow end-to-end, the slot is taken.
3. **Channel judo — sell to the liability-holder, not the broker.**
   Brokers are Quickli's ($99 already spent). The RISK/COMPLIANCE officers at
   non-bank lenders + aggregators have budget and rising pain (ADM disclosure Dec
   2026, AML, BID audits). Sell forensics + compliance-evidence as a RISK product,
   or white-label "powered-by DebtIQ" to a lender (as Quickli powers Thinktank/RedZed).
   KILL: if 2 non-bank risk officers say "Fortiro already covers us," done.
4. **Ride the 2026 compliance wave (timing/regulation-forced buying).**
   ADM disclosure (Dec 2026), AML tranche-2, CDR-NBL, BNPL ACL — a stack of new
   obligations generalist CRMs are slow to operationalize. Ship compliance-first,
   explainable, audit-ready modules timed to the wave.
   KILL: if Mercury Nexus / Salestrekker ship ADM + AML modules first.
5. **Orchestration layer ("Switzerland").**
   Don't beat Quickli/Fortiro/Frollo — integrate them by API and be the unifying
   compliance/assessment brain on top. Partner-led, rides their adoption.
   KILL: if none expose usable APIs, or they close them.
6. **Commercial / SME vertical (the thin gap from the prior file).**
   No Quickli-grade serviceability for commercial; SMEs ≈ 98% of businesses. Needs
   an engine rebuild. KILL: if 2 commercial aggregators say current tools are fine.

## The modified Rainmaker verdict
The winnable game is the NEW category (agentic + CDR-native + compliance-wave), not
the OLD one (serviceability tool). Incumbency protects the old board, not the new
one. BUT: it is a race with a clock (Quickli, Bulma, NextGen, global agents are all
moving), so the discipline is — pick ONE seam, pick a buyer who is NOT already
covered, and prove it in weeks, not quarters. "Modify the outlook" = stop fighting
for the broker's $99 in the old category; go win the emerging one.

## Two-week test (parallelised, cheap)
- Seam 1/3: show the unified flow to 5 brokers AND email 2 non-bank risk officers
  ("would the risk team pay for verified-docs + an auditable BID trail?").
- Seam 2: confirm whether NextGen/Frollo already own broker CDR+action-initiation.
- Seam 6: ask 2 commercial aggregators what serviceability/compliance tool they wish
  existed.
First seam to return a paying "yes" (or a corp-dev "let's talk") wins the roadmap.
