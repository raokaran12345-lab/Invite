# Evidence — Australian credit ecosystem map + competitive reality (2026-06-06)

Type: REAL market research (cited web sources). Maps the AU credit value chain to
find DebtIQ's buyers, channels, and — critically — who already owns each piece of
its intended wedge. This CORRECTS the earlier, more optimistic "open wedge" read.
Confidence per claim; exact figures ±, direction solid (some publisher pages 403
on direct fetch, figures from search extraction).

## The uncomfortable headline
DebtIQ's four pillars are each already owned, contested, or structurally shrinking:
1. **Serviceability** → owned by **Quickli** (~13–14k of ~19–22k brokers, AI-enabled). [high]
2. **Document extraction / OCR** → being obsoleted by **CDR/open banking** for bank
   data, and already productised by **Fortiro Accelerate** and open-banking players. [high]
3. **Document forensics / fraud detection** → already owned by **Fortiro** (VC-backed,
   sells to "Australia's leading banks, non-bank lenders, insurers") and **OziShield**
   (Sydney; 27 forensic checks incl. ABN/PAYG/super/BSB). This is NOT the open wedge
   the first report implied. [high]
4. **NCCP / Best-Interests-Duty responsible-lending EVIDENCE trail** → the LEAST
   contested sliver. None of Fortiro (fraud), FrankieOne (identity/AML), Quickli
   (serviceability) or the open-banking players produce the broker-side BID/suitability
   narrative + audit trail. But it's narrow and overlaps aggregator CRMs. [medium]

## The single most important competitor: Fortiro
- Melbourne fintech, founded 2021, VC-backed (OIF Ventures), 2024 "Startup of the Year".
- Products: **Accelerate** (extract+verify income/expenses from payslips & bank
  statements), **Protect** (automate document-fraud checks, remove manual review),
  **Redact**. Sells to banks, **non-bank lenders**, insurers, payments. [high]
- This is DebtIQ's extraction+forensics value prop, already built, funded, and selling
  to the exact lender buyers DebtIQ would target. Treat as the incumbent in the wedge.

## Structural threat: CDR is eating the document layer
- CDR expands to **non-bank lenders from 13 Jul 2026** (product data), consumer data
  9 Nov 2026 / 10 May 2027; **BNPL covered**. [high]
- Open banking (Frollo, illion/Experian, Basiq, Fiskil) already delivers **bank-verified
  income/expense data via API** — "the document layer died" (MogoPlus). As CDR spreads,
  OCR-and-forensically-checking a forgeable BANK STATEMENT loses its reason to exist. [high]
- Surviving document niche: payslips, tax returns/NOAs, BAS, employment letters are NOT
  in CDR, and self-employed / alt-doc / SME borrowers still rely on documents; GenAI
  document fraud rose ~244% in 2024. So forensics on NON-bank-statement docs retains
  demand — but that is exactly where Fortiro/OziShield already play. [medium-high]

## The ecosystem (buyers / channels / adjacent) — who relates to credit
- **Aggregators / CRMs** (channel + compliance liability): AFG (Flex), Finsure
  (Infynity — "CRM and compliance tools"), Connective (Mercury Nexus), LMG, Loan Market,
  Mortgage Choice/REA. [high]
- **Banks / ADIs**: majors + regionals + mutuals — already served by Fortiro/FrankieOne. [high]
- **Non-bank / specialist lenders** (booming — 84% of brokers use them, record high):
  Pepper, Liberty, Resimac, Thinktank, La Trobe, Bluestone, ORDE, RedZed, MA Money,
  Pallas, Assetline. Carry credit risk + AUSTRAC AML duty → fraud/verification buyers. [high]
- **SME / business lenders**: Prospa (now to $1m), Lumi, Moula, Banjo, Shift. Alt-lending
  ≈ A$33.6bn by 2029. Fast credit decisions need verification. [medium]
- **Consumer / personal / BNPL**: Wisr, MONEYME, Plenti, Latitude; Afterpay, Zip, Klarna.
  **BNPL has needed an ACL since 10 Jun 2025** → new compliance burden = potential buyers. [high]
- **Origination / decisioning software (the rails)**: NextGen/ApplyOnline (~98% of
  broker-originated mortgages), Sandstone, Loanworks. Partner-or-compete, not standalone-beat. [high]
- **Identity / AML / KYC regtech**: FrankieOne (orchestrates 350+ sources; CBA, Westpac,
  Afterpay), GBG/GreenID. Owns onboarding compliance. [high]
- **Open-banking / data**: Frollo, illion (Experian), Basiq, Fiskil. [high]
- **Credit bureaus**: Equifax, Experian, illion. **Property/valuation**: CoreLogic, PropTrack.
  **ELNO/settlement**: PEXA, Sympli. **Comparison/lead-gen**: Canstar, Finder, RateCity. [med]

## What this means (honest)
- The "horizontal AI co-pilot that does OCR + forensics + serviceability + compliance"
  positioning collides head-on with Quickli (serviceability), Fortiro (forensics/extraction),
  and CDR (document layer). As a standalone product fighting all three, it is weak.
- The defensible options are NARROWER than hoped:
  (A) **Compliance-evidence layer** — the BID/responsible-lending suitability trail nobody
      else makes — sold to aggregators/lenders who carry the liability. Least contested.
  (B) **Alt-doc / self-employed / SME document segment** — forensics + extraction on the
      docs CDR doesn't cover — but compete with Fortiro/OziShield there.
  (C) **Be a feature/partner, not a platform** — integrate into NextGen/Sandstone or an
      aggregator CRM; or partner with (even be acquired by) Fortiro/an aggregator.
- Willingness-to-pay is real (Quickli ~$99/mo × 13k; Fortiro sells to banks), so money
  exists — but it accrues to whoever owns a SPECIFIC job, and DebtIQ does not yet own one.

## Revised cheapest first test
Before more building, validate the ONLY likely-unowned job: take the **BID /
responsible-lending compliance-evidence trail** to (a) 2–3 aggregator compliance heads
and (b) 5 brokers, and ask: "Would you pay for an audit-ready Best-Interests-Duty +
responsible-lending evidence trail, given you already have Quickli for serviceability and
your aggregator CRM for compliance?" If the answer is no, the honest conclusion may be
partner/integrate (or pivot the asset), not build a standalone. Also directly ask 2
non-bank lenders whether they already use Fortiro — if yes, the forensics wedge is closed.
