# Evidence — Acquire/licence shortlist + niche re-aim (2026-06-06)

Type: REAL market research (cited). Answers: which buyers would licence/acquire
DebtIQ's assets, and what the cleanest independent re-aim is. Confidence per claim.

## Strong precedent: aggregators DO buy broker software
- **AFG (ASX-listed) ACQUIRED BrokerEngine** (workflow + compliance automation) on
  31-Aug-2023. Direct precedent for the acqui-hire/licence path. [high]
- **LMG** (largest aggregator, ~6,000+ brokers) acquired **The Brokers' Bible**
  (Jul 2025) and is building its own tech (MyCRM). Acquisitive. [high]
- **MA Financial** bought **Finsure** for ~A$145m (2022); **REA Group** owns
  **Mortgage Choice** (~1,100 brokers) — both deep-pocketed, listed/ecosystem
  buyers. [high]
So a small, differentiated broker-tech asset is a sellable thing — this is a
proven exit shape in this exact market.

## Acquire / licence shortlist (ranked by appetite + fit)
1. **AFG** — proven acquirer of broker compliance/workflow software (BrokerEngine);
   ASX-listed cash; would want AI compliance+forensics to extend. **Top target.**
2. **LMG** — largest, acquisitive, building MyCRM; wants differentiation. **Strong.**
3. **REA / Mortgage Choice** — deep pockets, ecosystem play, wants tech edge.
4. **Platform vendors racing to add AI compliance/forensics** — natural buyers of
   the IP to leapfrog: **Salestrekker 2.0** (80,000 dev hrs; serves SFG/Vow/Outsource/
   Purple Circle), **Effi** (integrated with 98% of aggregator software), possibly
   **Quickli** (expanding into docs) or **Fortiro** (regtech consolidation). [medium]
5. **Non-bank lenders** (Pepper et al.) — but Pepper already uses Fortiro, so the
   fraud slot is filled; lower odds.
Reality check: most SMALLER aggregators are renting the gap (adopting Salestrekker/
Effi), so they won't buy — the buyers are the big acquisitive aggregators (AFG/LMG/
REA) and the platform vendors. [high]

## Niche re-aim — two options, ranked
A) **Commercial / SME finance broking = the cleaner gap.** [medium-high]
   - SMEs ≈ 98% of Australian businesses; brokers are expanding into commercial/
     asset finance; demand is large and growing.
   - Tooling is THINNER and less saturated than residential — MyCRM, Platform
     Finance, Cloudcase (UBOO), HES LoanBox exist, but there is **no Quickli-grade
     serviceability tool for commercial** and compliance is more manual.
   - Quickli and Fortiro are residential-focused, so competition is weaker here.
   - COST: DebtIQ's engine is built for residential NCCP/APRA; commercial
     serviceability is non-standardised and BID/responsible-lending applies
     differently — this is a real rebuild, not a re-skin. High upside, high effort.
B) **Residential alt-doc / self-employed.** [medium]
   - Less eroded by CDR (BAS, tax returns, accountant declarations, employment
     letters are NOT in open banking), so document extraction+forensics stays
     relevant longer.
   - BUT Fortiro already extracts tax assessments/BAS + ABN checks, and Quickli Pro
     has an alt-doc/specialist matching tool — so it's contested on both axes.
   - Verdict: "less bad than PAYG," but not uncontested.

## The honest Rainmaker conclusion (capstone)
- **Fastest realistic money for the founder: package DebtIQ as an acqui-hire /
  licence** to AFG, LMG, REA, or a platform vendor (Salestrekker/Effi/Quickli/
  Fortiro). The differentiated story to sell: "AI document forensics + automated
  responsible-lending/BID evidence + a multi-lender serviceability engine — buy it
  to leapfrog." Precedent (AFG→BrokerEngine, LMG→Brokers' Bible) says this exits.
- **Best independent pivot: commercial/SME broking** — thinner competition, huge
  TAM, but budget for a real rebuild of the engine.
- **Weakest path: standalone residential AU broker SaaS** — owned on every axis.

## Cheapest test (do before building anything new)
1. Email **AFG and LMG corporate development**: "We have an AI compliance+forensics+
   serviceability module — is this a build/partner/buy conversation?" (1 reply tells
   you if the exit is real.)
2. Talk to **2 commercial/asset-finance aggregators** (Platform Finance, SME-focused
   groups): "Is there a serviceability/compliance tool you wish existed for
   commercial?" (validates the pivot niche.)
3. Ask **1 platform vendor** (Salestrekker/Effi): partner or compete?
A single "yes, let's talk" from #1 is worth more than another month of building.
