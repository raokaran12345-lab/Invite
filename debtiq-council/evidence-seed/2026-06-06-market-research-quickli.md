# Evidence — Market research: the "beat Quickli" thesis (2026-06-06)

Type: REAL market research (cited, web sources). This is genuine external ground
truth about competitors, market size, and willingness-to-pay — usable by the
council to move the MARKET dimension, not just product. Confidence noted per claim.
Caveat: several publisher sites (theadviser, mpamag, quickli.com.au,
fintechaustralia, australianfintech) block direct fetch (HTTP 403); the figures
below come from search-engine extraction of those same pages, so treat exact
numbers as ±, the direction as solid.

## Headline finding (this reframes the strategy)
The founder's instinct — "don't be ApplyOnline, beat Quickli" — is HALF right:
- **Right:** don't fight ApplyOnline. NextGen's ApplyOnline processes ~98% of all
  broker-originated mortgages / ~70% of ALL Australian mortgages, 60+ lenders,
  1M+ transactions/yr. It is the lodgement monopoly. Head-on = unwinnable. [high]
- **Wrong/outdated:** Quickli is no longer a soft target. It is the entrenched
  category winner in broker serviceability — ~13,000–14,000 brokers (more than
  HALF of Australia's ~19,000–22,000 brokers), ~50 lender calculators in one UI,
  2025 "Industry Supplier of the Year" (FBAA). Beating it head-on at
  serviceability is now its own uphill battle. [high]
- **Critical:** Quickli is ALREADY moving onto DebtIQ's intended turf. Its 2025
  Pro tier ($99/user/mo +GST; $89 multi-user) added its first AI: "Jiffi AI" (an
  AI credit analyst answering policy/product across ~45 lenders), "Doc Renamer"
  (auto-detect + rename client docs), and an alt-doc/specialist-lending matching
  engine + SMSF calculator. Pro hit ~1,500 registrations in 2 weeks. [high]

## What this means for DebtIQ's wedge
- Serviceability is now TABLE STAKES, not a wedge — Quickli owns it and is
  AI-enabling it. A serviceability clone does not win.
- BUT Quickli's document work is renaming + policy research, NOT forensic
  tamper-detection and NOT a structured NCCP/responsible-lending compliance
  evidence + audit trail. That specific wedge — "verified documents + a defensible
  Best-Interests-Duty / responsible-lending trail" — is still relatively
  unoccupied. [medium-high]
- So the defensible play is NOT "beat Quickli at serviceability." It is: lead with
  document forensics + compliance evidence (the thing brokers' and aggregators'
  LICENCES depend on), and treat serviceability as a supporting feature or an
  integration — not the headline. [medium, strategic inference]

## Willingness-to-pay — GOOD NEWS, now evidenced
- Per-broker SaaS at this exact price band is PROVEN: Quickli Core ~$530–590/yr
  +GST; Pro ~$99/user/mo +GST, and 13k+ brokers pay. So "brokers will pay ~$50–
  $100/mo for a tool that saves prelim-assessment time" is no longer a hypothesis
  — the market clears here. [high] This de-risks DebtIQ's monetization model.
- Channel proof: lenders white-label Quickli's calculators (Thinktank, RedZed
  "powered by Quickli"). A lender/aggregator embed channel exists and pays. [high]

## Distribution / gatekeeping
- Aggregators provide the CRM + compliance tooling brokers already use — AFG
  (Flex), Finsure (Infynity, explicitly "CRM and compliance tools"), Connective
  (Mercury Nexus). Compliance is increasingly THEIR feature and THEIR liability —
  which is a reason the compliance wedge may sell better to aggregators/lenders
  than to individual brokers. [medium]
- Brokers write ~77% of new home loans (MFAA, 2025) — the channel is big and
  growing; the constraint is reaching them, not demand for broking. [high]

## New Zealand (secondary market)
- Advisers now arrange ~half of new NZ mortgages (up from ~35%) — a fast-growing
  channel. [medium]
- Regulatory TAILWIND for a compliance wedge: final AML/CFT phase effective 1 Jun
  2025 (risk-rating + CDD record-keeping), plus CCCFA affordability/suitability
  scrutiny. That is exactly what a "verified docs + compliance evidence" product
  addresses. [medium]
- Smaller market (NZ ~5M vs AU ~26M); treat as a secondary beachhead AFTER an AU
  signal, not a pivot. [high]

## Verdict on the thesis
"Beat Quickli with serviceability + forensics/compliance" — as stated, NO: you do
not out-serviceability the 60%-share incumbent that is now AI-enabling. The
salvageable, sharper thesis: **win the compliance/verification job Quickli and
ApplyOnline don't own — document tamper-detection + a defensible NCCP/BID evidence
trail — sold per-broker (price band proven) and especially to aggregators/lenders
who carry the compliance liability.** Serviceability is the supporting act.

## Cheapest first test (do this before more building)
Take ONLY the forensics + compliance-evidence module to (a) 10 brokers and (b)
1–2 aggregator/lender compliance teams. One question: "Would you pay $X/mo for
verified documents + a defensible responsible-lending/BID audit trail, on top of
your existing serviceability tool?" Capture yes/no, price, and whether the buyer
is the broker or the aggregator. That single test resolves the buyer, the price,
and the wedge at once.
