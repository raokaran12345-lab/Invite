# Evidence — Compliance-path reality + partner/acquire options (2026-06-06)

Type: REAL market research (cited). Resolves the two follow-ups: (a) is the
BID/responsible-lending compliance-evidence path actually open? (b) who would
partner/white-label/acquire DebtIQ's assets? Confidence per claim; figures ±.

## (a) The compliance-evidence path is largely CLOSED for a standalone broker product
The "unowned sliver" from the prior report is, on inspection, already owned —
mostly bundled into the CRMs brokers already use through their aggregator:
- **Mercury Nexus (Connective) "Compliance Centre"**: automations/workflows for
  **BID + NCCP**, keeps an **audit log of all changes for audit file review**, and
  **prepopulates compliance documents** — i.e. exactly DebtIQ's "defensible
  audit-ready BID trail," already shipped and bundled. [high]
- **Infynity (Finsure)**: CRM + marketing + **compliance** in one platform. [high]
- **Salestrekker 2.0**: now embedding **AI across broker workflows to strengthen
  compliance** — "automate document verification and compliance checks" and
  "audit-ready client files," rolling out with the SFG aggregator. This is
  DebtIQ's exact pitch, shipping now. [high]
- **Effi**: AI document management, integrated with **98% of aggregator software**,
  SOC2. **BrokerEngine**: compliance workflow. [medium-high]
Verdict: audit-ready BID/NCCP compliance files + AI document verification is a
CROWDED, actively-AI-ed, mostly-bundled category. A standalone DebtIQ selling this
to brokers is late to a feature they already get from their CRM. [high]

## Fortiro confirmed as the forensics+extraction incumbent (with a Pepper case study)
Fortiro extracts income from payslips/bank statements/tax assessments, "detects 3X
more fraud," does ABN lookups + name matching + policy checks, integrates with LOS,
ISO 27001, and explicitly frames around **responsible lending** ("Liar Loans,
Responsible Lending and Document Fraud"). Live customer: **Pepper Money**. DebtIQ's
extraction+forensics is fully covered here. [high]

## Structural headwind reconfirmed
CDR to non-bank lenders from 13 Jul 2026; aggregators already turning on open
banking (Finsure+Frollo+NextGen "Financial Passport" = bank-verified data inside
ApplyOnline). The document/OCR layer keeps shrinking. [high]

## (b) Partner / integrate / acquire — the realistic money path, and the market is
##     already consolidating this way
- **Aggregators acquire point fintechs**: LMG agreed to buy **The Brokers' Bible**
  (Jul 2025, a lender-policy fintech). Aggregators are "evolving into key technology
  enablers." So a small, differentiated tech asset CAN be sold to an aggregator. [high]
- **NextGen/ApplyOnline is the integration hub, not a thing you replace**: lenders
  (Skip, OwnHome, Brighten, Thinktank) integrate INTO ApplyOnline; it connects to
  all aggregator CRMs and bundles Financial Passport + NextGenID + Access Seeker.
  The path is "integrate as a component," and the obvious components (fraud, open
  banking, IDV) are already filled (Fortiro, Frollo, NextGenID). [high]
- **Industry consensus**: "vendor-to-vendor collaboration and coordinated product
  development may reshape the market more than acquisition alone"; "RegTech entering
  its age of consolidation." Standalone challengers are NOT the winning shape. [medium]

## The honest Rainmaker verdict
DebtIQ-as-a-standalone-venture faces an already-owned competitor on EVERY pillar —
Quickli (serviceability), Fortiro (forensics+extraction, incl. Pepper), Mercury
Nexus / Salestrekker 2.0 / Effi (AI compliance files), NextGen (rails) — plus the
CDR headwind. There is no pillar DebtIQ uniquely owns. So the money paths, ranked:
1. **Sell the asset/team to an aggregator or broker-platform** wanting to leapfrog
   on AI compliance+forensics (LMG/SFG/smaller aggregators without strong tech) —
   licence or acqui-hire. Most realistic near-term "money for the founder". 
2. **Re-aim at a less-contested niche**: alt-doc / self-employed / SME document
   segment CDR doesn't cover, or commercial/asset finance where tooling is thinner —
   but still vs Fortiro.
3. **Geography**: take the engine somewhere with less entrenched incumbents (NZ
   smaller but compliance tailwind; or a market with no Quickli/Fortiro equivalent).
4. **Standalone AU broker SaaS**: weakest path — owned on every axis.

## Cheapest test that resolves "is there money here at all" (do FIRST, ~5 calls)
- 2 **aggregators** (e.g. an SFG-/LMG-tier group without best-in-class tech):
  "Would you licence or acquire an AI compliance + document-forensics module?"
- 2 **non-bank lenders**: "Do you already use Fortiro? If not, would you pay for X?"
- 1 **origination platform / NextGen**: "Is there an integration slot DebtIQ fills
  that Fortiro/Frollo/NextGenID don't?"
If these are no, the honest answer is asset-sale / pivot, not standalone build.
